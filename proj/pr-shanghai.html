food policentrality

Shanghai is developing rapidly, and it is already the biggest city in the world in terms of population. As a result of the long-term immigration, city suburbs have already started to cluster, which has brought the city government to work on a masterplan for the future development of this recent polycentric logic. Based on the fact that a city is the place where daily activities take place, ‘Food Policentrality’ seeks to detect and measure the existing levels of centralities from a food-perspective and aims to propose different urban food recipes to foster a characterization of these areas.
This research has used data from Tripadvisor, Meituan, Dianping and Bonapp and isolated 5 major variables: food pricing and quality, user’s satisfaction level, density of food places to go and diversity of cuisine offered. Food centers are detected through different spatial analysis techniques, frequency analysis through histograms and normalization techniques.
